பமலும், சந்பதைத்திற்ைிடமரன நபர்ைள், குழுவினர்ைளிடமிந்து வரைனங்ைள் மற்றும் தங்ைளது பசரத்துை்ைள் பதரடர்பரை மை்ைள் விழிப்புடன் இருை்குமரறும், சந்பதைத்திற்ைிடமரன தைவல் ஏபதனும் இருப்பின் அருைிலுள்ள பபரலிஸ் நிகலயத்திற்கு உடனடியரை அறிவிை்குமரறும், பபரலிஸரர் பபரதுமை்ைளிடம் பைட்டுை்பைரள்ைின்றனர்.

பபரதுமை்ைளின் பரதுைரப்பு மற்றும் நலனுை்ைரை பபரலிஸரர் பமற்பைரள்ளும் இை்ைடகமைளுை்கு, பபரதுமை்ைளின் பதரடர்ச்சியரன ஒத்துகழப்கப இலங்கை பபரலிஸரர் எதிர்பரர்ை்ைின்றனர்.

Implementation of a Comprehensive Security and Traffic Plan for Christmas and New Year Festivities – Police Headquarters

The Inspector General of Police (IGP) has instructed all Senior Deputy Inspector Generals in charge of provinces, as well as all Officers-in-Charge (OICs) of police stations island-wide, to implement a comprehensive security program and traffic management plan to ensure the safety of cities and religious sites during the upcoming Christmas and New Year celebrations.

In view of the festive season, a structured security and traffic plan has been formulated and put into operation to safeguard the public visiting major cities for various purposes and to facilitate the smooth conduct of their activities without inconvenience.

As special religious services and related programs will be held in Catholic churches across the island to commemorate Christmas, necessary security measures have been arranged in coordination with religious leaders and event organizers to ensure the safety of all devotees.

Enhanced security arrangements have also been deployed in areas where large crowds are expected, including financial institutions, commercial establishments, and shopping areas.

Furthermore, intelligence officers and police personnel in plain clothes have been stationed in urban areas to gather intelligence and take prompt preventive action where necessary.

Special attention has also been given to the security of hotels, shopping malls, and locations frequently visited by both local and foreign tourists.

Accordingly, the Police request the public to remain vigilant regarding suspicious individuals, groups, vehicles, or objects, and to immediately report any such observations to the nearest police officer.

The Sri Lanka Police continually seek the cooperation and support of the public in carrying out these duties to ensure public safety, security, and convenience.
