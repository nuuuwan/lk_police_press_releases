Security and Traffic Arrangements for the 2026 T-20 World Cup Match Between India and Pakistan

Comprehensive security and traffic management plans have been formulated for the 2026 T-20 World Cup match between India and Pakistan. All necessary measures have been implemented to ensure public safety, traffic regulation, parking arrangements, and the introduction of alternative routes. Adequate preparations have also been made to facilitate the convenience and safety of spectators.

The Sri Lanka Police, the Police Special Task Force, the Tri-Forces, and intelligence agencies are jointly executing the required security operations and traffic control plans. Approximately 2,000 police officers and nearly 600 traffic personnel have been deployed to provide security and manage traffic for the match scheduled on 15.02.2026.

The India–Pakistan match will be held at the R. Premadasa International Cricket Stadium and is scheduled to commence at 7:00 a.m. on 15.02.2026. Stadium gates will be opened to spectators at 3:00 p.m. The organizing committee has announced that all tickets have been sold out. Accordingly, only valid ticket holders will be permitted entry to the stadium.

Members of the public who have not obtained tickets are advised to watch the match from their homes via television broadcast. Spectators without tickets are strongly discouraged from gathering in the vicinity of the stadium in anticipation of acquiring tickets. Both foreign visitors and local supporters are kindly requested to conduct themselves with restraint, mutual respect, and sportsmanship.

Spectators attending the match are requested not to bring plastic or glass bottles into the stadium premises. Adequate drinking water facilities will be available inside the venue. The police will strictly enforce the law against individuals who are intoxicated, disorderly, or engage in inappropriate behavior.

Additionally, large bags, helmets, jackets, alcoholic beverages, and musical instruments will not be permitted inside the stadium. The use or possession of drones, sharp objects such as knives, and unauthorized electronic devices is strictly prohibited.

Vehicular access will be restricted at the Kettarama Mawatha Junction and from Wilson Perera Mawatha to the Britrobaba Pulle Junction. Entry through these points will be permitted only to vehicles bearing authorized passes issued by the Cricket Board. No other vehicles will be allowed access.

Designated parking areas have been arranged for public convenience, including the Stadium Parking Grounds, Sathosa Parking Area, Sebastian Ela Road Parking Area, Mati Park Parking Area, Kovila Road Parking Area, Applewatte Parking Area, and both sides of the 100-Foot Road.

Traffic restrictions will be in effect along Vincent Perera Mawatha, Grandpass Road, Brittobaba Pulle Road, Sebastian Ela Road, the Melwatte Bridge entrance to the stadium, 100-Foot Road, Temple Road Junction entrance to the stadium, Maligawatte Lane, the side road entrance to the stadium, and from Saddharma Mawatha–Kettarama Road Junction to Temple Road.

All spectators are requested to extend their fullest cooperation to the security personnel, as CCTV monitoring, body searches, and vehicle inspections will be conducted prior to and during the event to ensure the safety of all attendees.
