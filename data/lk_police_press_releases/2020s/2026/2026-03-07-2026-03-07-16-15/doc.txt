International Women’s Day – 2026.03.08

“Our tribute to the woman who conquers the world with courage”

Today marks International Women’s Day, and we extend our heartfelt gratitude and appreciation to women across the world. Every year on March 8, the world celebrates this day to recognize the extraordinary contributions of women and to draw global attention to the challenges they continue to face. The Sri Lanka Police proudly joins the global community in commemorating International Women’s Day 2026, under the theme “The Aspiration for a Prosperous Nation – The Pathway of Women.”

International Women’s Day is not merely a commemorative event but a reflection of the struggles, sacrifices, and achievements of women throughout human history. The origins of this day can be traced to the 1908 movement for the rights of working women in New York, United States. Subsequently, at an international conference in Denmark, Clara Zetkin proposed establishing a dedicated day to advocate for women’s rights worldwide. As a result, several European countries began celebrating this day from 1911 onwards. Although the concept of Women’s Day emerged internationally, it gained wider recognition in Sri Lanka after the United Nations (UN) officially declared March 8 as International Women’s Day in 1975.

International Women’s Day recognizes the social, economic, cultural, and political achievements of women. Each year, a major national ceremony is organized in Sri Lanka to mark this occasion, during which women who have made outstanding contributions in various sectors are honored and awarded.

Sri Lanka Police Service and Its Contribution to Women

Examining the history of the Sri Lanka Police, the recruitment of women into the police service began for the first time in 1952. The inaugural group consisted of several female Sub-Inspectors and Women Police Constables. Since then, women officers

In terms of services provided for the protection and welfare of women:

• The Sri Lanka Police maintains several specialized units dedicated to safeguarding women’s rights and ensuring their safety.

• Children and Women’s Bureau: Established in almost every police station, this bureau investigates cases related to violence against women, domestic abuse, and child protection. It also provides a supportive and secure environment where women victims can express their concerns with confidence.

• Women Police Officers also conduct community awareness programmes at the village level and organize safety education programmes for schoolchildren.
